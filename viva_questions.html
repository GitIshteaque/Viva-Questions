Tally (ERP) Viva Questions

‚ÑπÔ∏è Tally is a powerful accounting software used by businesses for financial management, GST, and inventory control. Below are common viva questions with detailed answers.
  1. What is Tally?
    Tally is a comprehensive business accounting software that enables organizations to record, manage, and analyze all types of financial transactions. It automates bookkeeping, inventory, payroll, and tax processes, making business accounting efficient, accurate, and compliant with statutory requirements. Tally is widely used for its simplicity, reliability, and ability to handle complex accounting tasks for businesses of all sizes.
  2. What is the latest version of Tally?
    The latest version is TallyPrime, which features a modern interface, improved navigation, and enhanced modules for GST, inventory, and reporting. TallyPrime is designed to be more user-friendly, supports multi-tasking, and offers advanced data security and remote access, making it a significant upgrade over previous versions like Tally ERP 9.
  3. How do you create a company in Tally?
    To create a company, select 'Create Company' from the Company Info menu, then enter details such as company name, address, financial year, and statutory features. This process sets up a new environment for managing all your business accounts, ledgers, and transactions in Tally, and you can later modify these details as needed.
  4. What is a ledger?
    A ledger is an account head in Tally where all transactions of a particular type (such as cash, sales, or expenses) are recorded. Ledgers help organize and track financial data for reporting and analysis, and each ledger belongs to a group (like Assets, Liabilities, Income, or Expenses) for better classification and control.
  5. What is a voucher in Tally?
    A voucher is a document or entry in Tally used to record a specific financial transaction, such as payments, receipts, sales, or purchases. Each voucher type corresponds to a different kind of business activity, and vouchers ensure that all transactions are properly documented and can be easily retrieved for auditing or reporting.
  6. Name different types of vouchers in Tally.
    Common voucher types include Payment, Receipt, Contra, Journal, Sales, Purchase, Debit Note, and Credit Note. Each type is used for a specific transaction and helps maintain accurate records. For example, Payment vouchers are for outgoing payments, while Receipt vouchers are for incoming funds.
  7. How do you enable GST in Tally?
    Press F11 to open Company Features, then enable Goods and Services Tax (GST). Enter GST details such as registration type, GSTIN, and tax rates. This makes Tally ready for GST-compliant accounting, invoicing, and returns, and allows you to generate GST reports and file returns directly from the software.
  8. What is a cost centre?
    A cost centre is a unit, department, or location within an organization to which costs are allocated for tracking and analysis. In Tally, cost centres help monitor expenses and profitability for different business segments, projects, or branches, enabling better financial control and decision-making.
  9. What is a cost category?
    A cost category is a way to group cost centres into different classifications, allowing for parallel tracking of costs. This is useful for businesses that want to analyze costs by multiple dimensions, such as projects and departments, and helps in detailed cost analysis and reporting.
  10. How do you create a ledger under a group?
    Go to Accounts Info ‚Üí Ledgers ‚Üí Create, select the appropriate group (like Sundry Debtors or Expenses), and enter the ledger details. Grouping ledgers helps organize accounts for better reporting, control, and compliance with accounting standards.
  11. What is the shortcut to create a ledger during voucher entry?
    Press Alt + C while entering a voucher to quickly create a new ledger without leaving the voucher screen. This saves time, streamlines data entry, and ensures that all necessary ledgers are available during transaction recording.
  12. How do you view the Day Book in Tally?
    Go to Gateway of Tally ‚Üí Display ‚Üí Day Book, or simply press F2. The Day Book shows all vouchers entered on a particular day, helping you review, verify, and audit daily transactions efficiently.
  13. What is the use of F11 in Tally?
    F11 opens the Company Features menu, where you can enable or configure features like inventory, taxation, payroll, and more. It customizes Tally to suit your business needs and ensures that only relevant modules are active for your company.
  14. How do you shut a company in Tally?
    Press Alt + F1 to shut the currently open company. This closes the company data and returns you to the company selection screen, ensuring data security and preventing unauthorized access to sensitive information.
  15. What is the shortcut to duplicate a voucher?
    Press Alt + 2 on a voucher to duplicate it. This is useful for quickly creating similar entries without re-entering all details, saving time and reducing errors in repetitive transactions.
  16. How do you print a report in Tally?
    On any report screen, press Alt + P to open the print menu. You can customize print settings, select the printer, and generate hard copies of reports for records, sharing, or compliance purposes.
  17. What is the use of Alt+C in Tally?
    Alt + C allows you to create a new ledger, group, or item instantly while entering data, making the workflow faster and more flexible. This feature is especially helpful when you need to add new accounts on the fly during voucher entry.
  18. How do you export data from Tally?
    Press Alt + E on a report to export data in formats like Excel, PDF, or XML. Exporting helps in sharing, analysis, backup, and integration with other software or compliance systems.
  19. What is the difference between a group and a ledger?
    A group is a collection of similar ledgers (like all expense accounts), while a ledger is an individual account. Groups help organize ledgers for better reporting, control, and compliance, whereas ledgers record specific transactions.
  20. How do you view the balance sheet in Tally?
    Go to Gateway of Tally ‚Üí Balance Sheet or press Ctrl + B. The balance sheet provides a snapshot of assets, liabilities, and equity at a given date, helping you assess the financial position of the business.

DBMS (MS Access) Viva Questions

‚ÑπÔ∏è DBMS and MS Access are essential for managing and analyzing data. Below are important viva questions with detailed answers for your exam.
  1. What is DBMS?
    A Database Management System (DBMS) is a software system that enables users to define, create, maintain, and control access to databases. It helps manage large amounts of data efficiently, ensuring data integrity, security, and easy retrieval. DBMS also supports data backup, recovery, and multi-user access, making it vital for modern data-driven applications.
  2. What is MS Access?
    MS Access is a database management tool from Microsoft that allows users to create, manage, and analyze databases with a graphical interface. It supports forms, queries, reports, and relationships, making database management accessible to non-programmers. MS Access is widely used for small to medium-sized databases and integrates well with other Microsoft Office applications.
  3. What is a table in MS Access?
    A table in MS Access is a structured set of data made up of rows and columns, where each row represents a record and each column represents a field. Tables are the foundation for storing all data in a database, and each table can be related to others for complex data management.
  4. What is a primary key?
    A primary key is a unique identifier for each record in a table. It ensures that no two records are identical and helps establish relationships between tables for data integrity. Primary keys are essential for indexing and fast data retrieval.
  5. What is a foreign key?
    A foreign key is a field in one table that links to the primary key of another table. It is used to create relationships and maintain referential integrity between tables, ensuring that data remains consistent and accurate across the database.
  6. What is a query?
    A query is a request for information from a database. In MS Access, queries are used to retrieve, filter, update, or delete data based on specific criteria, making data analysis and reporting easier. Queries can be saved and reused for regular data tasks.
  7. What is a form in MS Access?
    A form is a user-friendly interface in MS Access that allows users to enter, view, or edit data in tables. Forms can include controls like text boxes, buttons, and combo boxes for better data management, and they help ensure data is entered accurately and efficiently.
  8. What is a report in MS Access?
    A report is a formatted output of data from tables or queries, designed for printing or sharing. Reports help present data in a structured and visually appealing way for analysis or record-keeping, and can include charts, summaries, and calculations.
  9. How do you create a relationship between tables?
    In MS Access, go to Database Tools ‚Üí Relationships, then drag and drop fields (usually primary and foreign keys) between tables to establish connections. This helps maintain data consistency, enables relational queries, and enforces referential integrity.
  10. What is normalization?
    Normalization is the process of organizing data in a database to reduce redundancy and improve data integrity. It involves dividing large tables into smaller, related tables and defining relationships between them, which helps in efficient data storage and retrieval.
  11. What is a data type?
    A data type specifies the kind of data a field can hold, such as text, number, date, or currency. Choosing the correct data type ensures data is stored and processed correctly in the database, and helps prevent errors during data entry and analysis.
  12. What is a combo box in a form?
    A combo box is a drop-down list in a form that allows users to select a value from predefined options. It improves data entry accuracy, user experience, and helps standardize the data entered into the database.
  13. How do you set a primary key in MS Access?
    In table design view, select the field you want as the primary key and click the key icon. This ensures each record is unique, helps with table relationships, and improves data retrieval speed.
  14. What is referential integrity?
    Referential integrity is a set of rules that ensures relationships between tables remain consistent. It prevents orphan records, maintains data accuracy across related tables, and enforces valid data entry in relational databases.
  15. What is the difference between a form and a report?
    A form is used for data entry and editing, providing an interactive interface, while a report is used for presenting and printing data in a structured format. Both are essential for managing and sharing database information, but serve different purposes in the workflow.
  16. How do you import data into MS Access?
    Use the External Data tab and select the appropriate import option (Excel, CSV, etc.). Follow the prompts to map fields and import data into your tables, making it easy to bring in data from other sources.
  17. What is SQL?
    SQL (Structured Query Language) is a programming language used to manage and manipulate databases. In MS Access, SQL is used behind the scenes for queries and data operations, allowing for powerful data analysis and automation.
  18. What is a macro in MS Access?
    A macro is a tool in MS Access that automates repetitive tasks, such as opening forms, running queries, or generating reports. Macros help improve efficiency, reduce manual work, and can be triggered by events or user actions.
  19. How do you create a query to filter data?
    Use Query Design, add the relevant tables, and set criteria for the fields you want to filter. This allows you to extract specific data based on your requirements, and you can save queries for future use.
  20. What is the use of relationships in DBMS?
    Relationships connect tables in a database, enabling you to combine data from multiple sources and maintain data integrity. They are essential for relational database design, allowing for complex queries and accurate reporting.

E-Filing Viva Questions

‚ÑπÔ∏è E-Filing is the process of submitting tax and statutory documents online. Below are key viva questions with expanded answers for your exam.
  1. What is e-filing?
    E-filing is the process of submitting tax returns or other statutory documents electronically through government-approved portals. It is faster, more convenient, and reduces paperwork compared to manual filing, making compliance easier for individuals and businesses. E-filing also provides instant acknowledgment, online tracking, and helps in faster processing of refunds.
  2. Name two government portals for e-filing in India.
    The two main government portals are https://www.incometax.gov.in for income tax returns and https://www.gst.gov.in for GST returns. These platforms provide secure, user-friendly interfaces for online filing, allow users to check status, and offer various resources for taxpayers.
  3. What is ITR?
    ITR stands for Income Tax Return, a form used by taxpayers to declare their income, deductions, and taxes paid to the government. Filing ITR is mandatory for individuals and entities above a certain income threshold and helps in claiming refunds, carrying forward losses, and maintaining legal compliance.
  4. What is Form 16?
    Form 16 is a certificate issued by an employer to an employee, showing the total salary paid and the tax deducted at source (TDS) during the financial year. It is an essential document for filing income tax returns, as it provides proof of tax paid and helps in accurate computation of tax liability.
  5. What is a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)?
    A Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is an electronic form of a signature used to authenticate the identity of the sender of a document. It is required for certain types of e-filing, especially for companies and professionals, and ensures the security and authenticity of online submissions.
  6. What is the last date for e-filing income tax returns?
    The last date for e-filing income tax returns for individuals is usually 31st July of the assessment year, unless extended by the government. Timely filing helps avoid penalties, ensures smooth processing of refunds, and keeps you compliant with tax laws.
  7. What is Form 26AS?
    Form 26AS is a consolidated annual tax statement that shows details of tax deducted at source (TDS), tax collected at source (TCS), advance tax, and self-assessment tax paid by a taxpayer. It is available on the income tax portal and is important for verifying tax credits, ensuring that all taxes paid are correctly credited to your account.
  8. What is e-verification?
    E-verification is the process of electronically confirming the authenticity of a filed return using methods like Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or digital signature. It completes the filing process, is mandatory for the return to be processed, and helps prevent fraud or unauthorized filings.
  9. What are the advantages of e-filing?
    E-filing offers several advantages: it is quick, paperless, secure, and provides instant acknowledgment. It also allows easy correction of errors, online tracking of status, faster processing of refunds, and reduces the risk of data loss compared to manual filing.
  10. What documents are required for e-filing?
    Documents required include PAN, Aadhaar, Form 16, bank account details, investment proofs, and details of other income. Having these ready ensures accurate and hassle-free filing, and helps in claiming deductions and verifying tax credits.
  11. Can you revise an e-filed return?
    Yes, an e-filed return can be revised before the end of the relevant assessment year if any mistake or omission is discovered. This helps taxpayers correct errors, avoid penalties, and ensure that their tax records are accurate and up to date.
  12. What is the difference between e-filing and manual filing?
    E-filing is done online through government portals, offering speed, convenience, and digital records. Manual filing involves submitting paper forms physically, which is slower, more prone to errors, and does not provide instant acknowledgment or online tracking.
  13. What is the process to register on the income tax e-filing portal?
    To register, visit the income tax portal, click on 'Register', and fill in your PAN, personal details, and contact information. After verification, you can set a password and start using the portal for filing and tracking returns, checking refund status, and accessing tax-related services.
  14. What is the use of Aadhaar in e-filing?
    Aadhaar is used for e-verification of returns and for linking with PAN, which is mandatory for filing. It simplifies the authentication process, helps in faster processing of returns, and is required for certain online services on the tax portal.
  15. What is the penalty for late filing of ITR?
    A penalty is levied for late filing of ITR as per the Income Tax Act. The amount depends on the delay and the taxpayer's income, and late filing may also result in loss of certain benefits, such as carrying forward losses or claiming refunds.
  16. What is the use of PAN in e-filing?
    PAN (Permanent Account Number) is a unique identifier required for filing and tracking tax returns. It is used to link all tax-related information and transactions to a taxpayer, and is mandatory for most financial activities in India.
  17. What is the process to link PAN with Aadhaar?
    Login to the income tax portal, go to 'Link Aadhaar', enter your PAN and Aadhaar details, and submit the request. Linking is mandatory for filing returns, helps prevent tax evasion, and ensures that your tax records are accurate and up to date.
  18. What is the meaning of assessment year?
    The assessment year is the year following the financial year in which income is assessed and tax is paid. For example, income earned in 2023-24 is assessed in the assessment year 2024-25. It is important for determining tax filing deadlines and compliance.
  19. What is the process to download Form 26AS?
    Login to the income tax portal, go to 'View Form 26AS', select the assessment year, and download the statement. It helps verify tax credits before filing returns, and ensures that all taxes paid are properly credited to your account.
  20. What is the use of pre-filled ITR forms?
    Pre-filled ITR forms automatically populate your personal and tax details from government records, reducing manual entry and errors. They make the filing process faster, more accurate, and help in avoiding mistakes while submitting returns.
